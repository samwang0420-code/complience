## Sacramento Roof Repair FAQ

**### How quickly can you respond to a roof leak emergency?**
We prioritize emergency calls, especially during and after major storm events. We aim to provide temporary tarping within 24-48 hours to prevent further interior damage and schedule the permanent repair promptly.

**### Do you work with insurance companies on storm damage claims?**
Yes, we are experienced in working with all major insurance providers. We can help you navigate the claims process, provide detailed documentation and photos of the damage, and work directly with your adjuster to ensure your roof repair is covered.

**### How can I tell if my roof has hail damage?**
Signs include random patterns of dark spots (bruising) on shingles, loss of asphalt granules (which will collect in gutters), and dings on metal flashing, vents, or gutters. After a suspected hailstorm, we recommend a professional inspection, as some damage can be subtle but still compromise your roof’s lifespan.

**### Why is attic ventilation so important for my Sacramento roof?**
Proper ventilation regulates attic temperature and moisture. In summer, a hot attic can cause shingles to blister and degrade prematurely. In winter, a warm attic can melt snow (or ice during rare cold snaps), leading to water refreezing at the eaves (ice dams) which can force water under shingles. We ensure your intake (soffit) and exhaust (ridge/roof vents) are balanced and unobstructed.

**### Can you just repair a section, or does my whole roof need to be replaced?**
We always recommend the most cost-effective, long-term solution. For localized damage from a fallen branch or isolated storm damage, a repair is often perfectly sufficient. However, if your roof is near the end of its lifespan (typically 20-25 years for asphalt shingles in our climate) or has widespread, multiple issues, we will honestly discuss if a replacement is a more prudent investment.

**### What sets your repairs apart for Sacramento’s "atmospheric river" storms?**
We build for intensity. This means using higher-grade sealants, implementing water-and-ice barrier protections in critical areas (like eaves and valleys) even during repairs, and ensuring all repairs are integrated to handle wind-driven rain. We understand how water seeks the path of least resistance and fortify those potential entry points.
