## How to Fix LG Washer Error Code PE: The Complete Troubleshooting Guide

Is your LG washer suddenly stopping mid-cycle, displaying a cryptic "PE" error code on its panel? This frustrating interruption signals a problem with your washer's ability to manage one of its most basic functions: water levels. Don't worry—while it requires some attention, the PE error is often diagnosable and fixable without an expensive service call. This comprehensive guide will explain what the LG washer error code PE means, explore its common causes, and provide detailed, step-by-step instructions to help you resolve it yourself.

### **Overview of LG Washer Error Code PE**

The LG washer error code PE stands for **"Pressure Sensor Error."** It is part of LG's self-diagnostic system, designed to alert you when the appliance cannot accurately detect or regulate the water level inside the drum. When the control board receives an unexpected or illogical signal from the pressure sensor system, it triggers the PE code, halts the cycle, and prevents potential overfilling or underfilling. This error can appear on a wide range of LG front-load and top-load washer models. Addressing it promptly is crucial, as ignoring it can lead to incomplete washes, water leakage, or further component strain.

### **What Does the LG Washer PE Error Code Mean? (Pressure Sensor Error)**

In simple terms, the PE error means your washer's "brain" (the main control board) is getting bad information from its "water level gauge" (the pressure sensor system). Unlike a simple float switch, modern LG washers use a sophisticated air-pressure system to determine water height.

Here’s how it’s supposed to work:
1.  As water enters the outer tub, it traps air in a chamber.
2.  This increasing water level compresses the air, which travels up a small plastic **pressure hose**.
3.  The compressed air reaches a **pressure sensor** (or pressure switch) mounted on the control panel or top frame.
4.  The sensor converts this air pressure into an electrical resistance signal, telling the control board exactly how much water is present.

A PE code appears when the signal from this system is absent, static, or doesn't correspond to the expected water level. The core meaning is a breakdown in this communication chain.

### **Common Causes of the LG Pressure Sensor Error PE**

Understanding the likely culprits will streamline your troubleshooting. The causes typically fall into three main categories, listed from most to least common:

1.  **Blocked, Kinked, or Disconnected Pressure Hose:** This is the #1 cause of the PE error. The small-diameter plastic tube can become clogged with lint, soap scum, or debris. It can also pop off its fittings on the sensor or the outer tub due to vibration, or become pinched behind the machine.
2.  **Faulty Pressure Sensor:** The electronic pressure sensor itself can fail. Internal components may wear out, become damaged by electrical surges, or simply fail due to age, providing incorrect resistance readings.
3.  **Air Leaks or Water in the Pressure System:** If the connection points of the hose are not airtight, or if the hose has a small crack, the system cannot build proper pressure. Similarly, if water siphons back into the hose (often due to a clog at the outer tub port), it can flood the sensor and cause a failure.
4.  **Failed Main Control Board:** While less common, it's possible for the main control board to malfunction and misinterpret correct signals from a functioning pressure sensor. This is typically the last component to check after ruling out all others.

### **Step-by-Step Guide: How to Fix LG Washer Error Code PE**

**⚠️ Safety First:** Always unplug your washer from the electrical outlet before starting any repair. Have towels and a container ready for potential water spillage.

#### **Step 1: Locate and Inspect the Pressure Hose**

The pressure hose is a thin, clear or grey plastic tube, usually about 1/4 inch in diameter.

*   **Access:** For most front-load models, you can access the hose by removing the top panel (pushing back the locking tabs at the rear) or by tilting the machine back. For top-load models, it's often accessible from the rear.
*   **Trace the Hose:** One end connects to the **pressure sensor** (mounted on a metal bracket, often near the control panel). The other end connects to the **outer tub**, usually at the top, via a plastic nipple or port.
*   **Visual Check:** Look for obvious disconnections, severe kinks, or cracks. Ensure both ends are snugly attached.
*   **Check for Clogs & Water:**
    *   Disconnect the hose from the sensor end first. Have a towel ready.
    *   Blow gently through the hose. You should feel little resistance. If it's blocked, you can try to clear it by blowing it out or using low-pressure compressed air.
    *   Look for water inside the hose. If water drips out, the clog is likely at the outer tub connection, which has allowed water to backflow.

#### **Step 2: Clean the Outer Tub Air Port**

If the hose was clogged or contained water, the port on the outer tub is likely blocked.

*   Disconnect the hose from the tub nipple.
*   You will see a small plastic port. This can become clogged with lint and residue.
*   Use a pipe cleaner, cotton swab, or small brush to gently clear any debris from the port. You can also flush it with warm water.
*   Reconnect the hose securely to the tub nipple.

#### **Step 3: Test the Pressure Sensor**

If the hose and port are clear, the sensor itself may be faulty.

*   **Disconnect:** Unplug the washer. Disconnect the pressure hose and the electrical connector from the sensor.
*   **Resistance Test (Requires a Multimeter):**
    *   Set your multimeter to measure resistance (Ohms, Ω).
    *   Attach the probes to the sensor's electrical terminals (consult your model's wiring diagram for the correct terminals, but often it's the middle and one outer pin).
    *   Note the initial resistance.
    *   **The Key Test:** Blow gently into the sensor's hose port. A **functioning sensor will show a smooth, gradual change in resistance** as you apply pressure. If the reading is infinite (open circuit), zero (short circuit), or jumps erratically, the sensor is defective and needs replacement.

#### **Step 4: Check for Air Leaks**

With everything reconnected, perform an air leak test.
*   Ensure every connection—sensor to hose, hose to tub—is tight and sealed.
*   Listen for a hissing sound when the washer is filling, which would indicate a leak.
*   You can use a soapy water solution on the connections and look for bubbles when pressure is in the system.
