## About AI Visibility & GEO

### What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

GEO is the practice of optimizing your medical practice for AI recommendation engines like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE). Unlike traditional SEO which focuses on search rankings, GEO ensures AI engines recommend your practice when patients ask for recommendations.

### How is GEO different from SEO?

| SEO | GEO |
|-----|-----|
| Targets search algorithms | Targets AI reasoning engines |
| Focuses on keyword rankings | Focuses on recommendation frequency |
| Success = #1 on Google | Success = AI recommends you |
| Backlinks are primary currency | Knowledge graph citations are primary |
| Content targets search queries | Content targets conversational questions |

### Why do I need GEO if I already rank #1 on Google?

47% of high-intent medical queries now start with AI engines, not traditional search. Even if you rank #1 on Google, you can be completely invisible to patients asking ChatGPT or Perplexity for recommendations. GEO captures that 47% of the market traditional SEO misses.

### How long does GEO take to show results?

Typical timeline:
- **Month 1-2:** Foundation building, minimal visible results
- **Month 3:** First AI referrals begin appearing
- **Month 4-6:** Significant referral volume
- **Month 6+:** Market-dominant position in most markets

Most practices see meaningful results within 90 days and substantial impact within 6 months.

## Technical Questions

### What is schema markup and why does it matter?

Schema markup is structured data that helps AI engines understand your practice. Medical-specific schema (MedicalOrganization, Physician, MedicalProcedure) tells AI engines:
- What services you offer
- Your credentials and specialties
- Your locations and contact information
- Your reviews and ratings

Without schema, AI engines struggle to accurately represent your practice in recommendations.

### What is llms.txt?

`llms.txt` is a documentation file that helps AI engines understand your services. Think of it as a "readme" file for AI crawlers. It documents:
- Your services and specializations
- Your physicians and credentials
- Your locations and contact information
- Key differentiators

### Do I need to change my website design?

Generally no. Most GEO improvements are technical (schema markup, content structure) rather than visual. However, we may recommend content additions or structural changes to improve AI readability.

### Will GEO affect my existing SEO?

GEO complements and enhances traditional SEO. Many GEO improvements (schema markup, content depth, authority building) also improve search rankings. You typically see improvements in both channels.

## Content & Authority

### What kind of content do I need to create?

GEO-optimized content includes:
- **Procedure deep-dives:** Comprehensive guides to your services
- **FAQ expansions:** Detailed answers to patient questions
- **Comparison content:** "Option A vs. Option B" analyses
- **Case studies:** Documented patient outcomes
- **Educational resources:** Pre/post care instructions

All content should be structured for AI readability with clear headings, semantic markup, and direct answers to common questions.

### How much content do I need?

Minimum viable:
- 10 procedure deep-dives (2,000+ words each)
- 100 FAQ answers
- 20 comparison articles
- 25 case studies

This provides sufficient authority signals for AI engines to recognize your expertise.

### Do I need to blog regularly?

Not necessarily. GEO focuses on comprehensive, evergreen content rather than frequent blogging. Quality and depth matter more than frequency. Many successful practices update content quarterly rather than weekly.

### Can AI write my content?

AI can assist with drafting, but human expertise is essential for:
- Medical accuracy and compliance
- Personal voice and perspective
- Patient-specific considerations
- Regulatory requirements

We recommend AI-assisted drafting with expert review and editing.

## Reviews & Reputation

### How important are reviews for GEO?

Critical. AI engines heavily weight review volume, recency, and sentiment when making recommendations. Practices with 200+ recent reviews significantly outperform those with fewer reviews.

### Which review platforms matter most?

Priority order:
1. **Google Business Profile** – Most important for local AI recommendations
2. **RealSelf** – Critical for aesthetics/plastic surgery
3. **Healthgrades** – Important for all medical practices
4. **Facebook** – Secondary but valuable
5. **Industry-specific** (RateMDs, Vitals, etc.)

### Should I respond to negative reviews?

Yes, professionally and promptly. AI engines analyze review responses as trust signals. A thoughtful response to a negative review often carries more weight than the negative review itself.

### Can I ask patients for reviews?

Yes, with important caveats:
- Never offer incentives (violates most platform policies)
- Ask at the right time (post-procedure, when satisfaction is highest)
- Make it easy (provide direct links)
- Follow up once, gently
