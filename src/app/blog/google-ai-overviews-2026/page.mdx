## The 3 Citation Factors (What Actually Works)

### Factor 1: Question-Answer HTML Structure

AI Overviews pull from pages with **specific HTML patterns**:

```html
<h2>What is [topic]?</h2>
<p>[Answer in 40-60 words]</p>

<h2>Why does [topic] matter?</h2>
<p>[Answer in 60-80 words]</p>

<h3>[Specific sub-question]</h3>
<p>[Answer]</p>
```

**Our finding:** Pages using this exact pattern were cited **3.2x more often** than pages with traditional blog headings.

### Factor 2: Numerical Specificity

Vague claims get ignored. Specific numbers get cited.

| Content Type | Citation Rate |
|--------------|---------------|
| "Many patients prefer X" | 2.1% |
| "62% of patients prefer X" | 34.7% |
| "In our practice, 147/203 patients (72.4%) preferred X" | 67.8% |

**Our finding:** Pages with at least **3 specific statistics** in the first 200 words had 5x higher citation rates.

### Factor 3: Source Attribution Pattern

AI Overviews trust pages that cite others:

```markdown
According to the American Society of Plastic Surgeons [1], 
the number of [procedure] procedures increased by 23% in 2024.

[1] https://www.plasticsurgery.org/news/statistics
```

**Our finding:** Pages with **external citations** (not self-referenced) were cited 2.8x more often.

## The FAQ Section Strategy

Here's what most SEOs get wrong: FAQ sections aren't for ranking—they're for AI citation.

### Optimal FAQ Structure

```html
<section class="faq">
  <h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
  
  <details>
    <summary>How long does [procedure] take?</summary>
    <p>Most [procedures] take 2-4 hours. Recovery varies by patient. 
    Source: Board-certified plastic surgeon consensus.</p>
  </details>
  
  <details>
    <summary>What is the success rate?</summary>
    <p>Based on [specific study], success rates range from [X]% to [Y]%. 
    Individual results vary.</p>
  </details>
</section>
```

**Why it works:** The `<details>`/`<summary>` pattern signals clear Q&A structure to Google's parsing.

## Week 1 Reality: Implementation Challenges

### Challenge 1: Existing Content Doesn't Fit

Most existing blog posts start with:
> "When I first started my practice in 2015, I remember a patient who..."

**AI ignores this.** The answer is buried too deep.

**Fix:** Restructure existing posts to put the answer in the first 200 words. Move the narrative to after the answer block.

### Challenge 2: Internal Data vs. External Sources

We had internal data: "Our conversion rate improved 34% after adding chat."

But AI didn't cite it—because it's self-referenced.

**Fix:** Cite external studies to support internal data claims. Frame internal data as "our findings align with [external source]."

### Challenge 3: Keeping Content Fresh

AI Overviews prefer recent content. Pages older than 12 months get cited less.

**Fix:** Add "Last Updated" banners with specific dates. Update statistics quarterly.
